Description of Source

B’Tselem, The Israeli Information Centre for Human Rights in the Occupied Territories believes in creating a future in which human rights, liberty and equality are guaranteed to all people, living between the Jordan River and the Mediterranean Sea. The name, B’Tselem, means in the image of in Hebrew. This was decided by the late Member of Knesset Yossi Sarid to express the universal and Jewish moral edict to respect and uphold the human rights of all people. They document and educate the public about human rights violations in the Israeli-occupied territories, including the West Bank, East Jerusalem, and Gaza Strip.

Field research, documentation, legal advocacy, and public outreach were methods to reveal and confront issues such as illegal executions, deprivation of property or land, and restrictions on freedom of movement. The organization has previously faced controversy and criticism where they are were accused of being biased against Israel or for undermining Israeli security. However, some argues that they play a crucial role in holding Israeli authorities accountable for their actions as well as encouraging respect for human rights and international law.

Information Available on Source

B’Tselem provides documentation of human rights violations. They conduct field research to record violations such as unlawful killings, excessive use of force, violence towards settlers, house demolitions, property confiscation, and restrictions on the rights to travel. They gather these violations with testimonies from victims and witnesses, collecting photographic and video evidence and compile data to support their findings.

Reports and publications are part of the information B’Tselem provides. They publish report papers and other publications based on research and documentation. The information provides in-depth analysis of human rights issues occurring in the territories, specifically highlighting the cases of abuse.

In addition, B’Tselem engages in legal advocacy to challenge human rights violations through Israeli courts as well as international human rights mechanisms. They would provide legal assistance to victims seeking compensation, filing petitions and lawsuits on behalf of either individuals or communities that have been affected.

This chosen source also raises awareness on issues regarding human rights. This is done through public outreach activities, such as interviews, press releases, social media campaigns, and public events. At the same time, they provide educational resources and information to schools and organisations to encourage better understanding of human rights principles and international law.

Collection of Data

B’Tselem collects data through various methods to document human rights violations in the Israeli-occupied territories. One of these approaches include field research. B’Tselem has a team of research and fieldworkers that carry out on-site investigations to gather information about related topics. They would visit affected areas to interview victims, witnesses and residents. Testimonies and evidence would also be collected, which includes photographs, videos and documents. Furthermore, they would collaborate with local organisations, activist, lawyers and community leaders to gather materials and share resources. They also would work with international human rights associations, journalists and researchers to exchange information and increase their impact.

Reliability of Source

A website named, ‘Media Bias / Fact Check’, promotes awareness and misinformation of media bias. It gives a detailed report on B’Tselem’s bias and credibility. The website rated B’Tselem as left-wing bias. They may use wordings that aims to influence readers’ emotions, publish misleading information and excludes any information that might have a negative impact on liberal causes. According to Media Bias/Fact Check, B’Tselem provides mostly factual reports with a medium credibility.

Another site described B’Tselem as ‘less reliability and credibility but more politics.’ Jerusalem Center for Public Affairs (JCPA) is an independent research institute producing studies and initiatives on a wide range of strategic topics. The article about B’Tselem suggested that they may be unfair and trustworthy due to their dependence on fundings and information from politically biased groups. The JCPA has their doubts on the legitimacy of B’Tselem’s way of observation and draws attention to their influence on anti-Israel campaigns.
