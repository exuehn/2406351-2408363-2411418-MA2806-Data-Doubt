Description of Source

Kaggle is often described as an AirBnB for data scientists. The website provides a platform for enthusiasts to refine their skills and tackle data science challenges in real-world setting. Kaggle started in Melbourne and later moved to Silicon Valley. They received several notable investors before being acquired by Google in 2017. The platform offers competitions ranging from predicting cancer occurrence to analysing how movies affect audience reaction, code sharing and dataset hosting. The community is fueled with high quality content and active engagement within users, making Kaggle the go-to platform for data scientists and machine learning engineers from all over the world.

Information Available on Source

Kaggle offers various resources and features for enthusiasts within the field. These include competitions hosted by Kaggle where participants are invited to solve challenging problems and projects. Furthermore, users on this platform can access an archive of dataset covering a variety of topic, allowing them to explore, learn and download to analyse data for research purposes. Kaggle also encourages users to be engage in the active community of data scientists that are open to collaborate, share insights and be asked questions through the forums. Other than this, useful tools are provided to help users improve their skills. These include, online tutorials, courses and learning paths covering topics such as data manipulation, data visualisation and machine learning algorithms.

Collection of Data

Kaggle access and collect data through various ways to create a diverse database for its users to access and analyse. The main method of data collection is by user contributions. Users are responsible for sourcing and uploading data from various external sources. By approaching this collaboratively, it allows a wide range of dataset from different origin, cultivating a rich and diverse collection of data available for Kaggle’s users. Users may implement data cleaning and preprocessing to enhance usefulness of the datasets before sharing them. Other than this, some of the dataset offered exclusively would be sourced externally. This may be from data providers and publicly available sources.

Reliability of Source

Regarding the reliability of Kaggle, there are websites and forums where users discuss their experiences and opinions on the datasets and competitions. However, there are no websites that solely focus on assessing Kaggle’s reliability. Most of the time, users on Kaggle rely on community feedback, ratings and reviews from others within Kaggle to determine the quality and reliability of the datasets.
